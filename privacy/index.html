Privacy Policy

Last updated: 1 July 2026
The short version. Chaotic Clock collects no data at all. No accounts, no analytics, no tracking, no ads. Your scores and streaks live only on your device, and the only thing that ever leaves it is a score you submit to Apple's Game Center — if you choose to sign in.

This policy explains how Chaotic Clock ("the game", "we") handles data. The data controller is Jordi Reina, contact: chaotic-clock@proton.me.

1. What we collect: nothing

The game contains no analytics or advertising SDKs and sends us no information. We do not collect your name, email, contacts, location, photos, gameplay data, device identifiers, or anything else — Chaotic Clock has no account system and no servers of its own.

2. Game Center

If you are signed in to Apple's Game Center, the game submits your scores to Apple so they can appear on leaderboards, and displays your Game Center profile as Apple provides it. Game Center is operated by Apple under Apple's privacy policy; we never see or store your Apple ID. You can disable Game Center in iOS Settings — the game works fine without it.

3. Data stored on your device

Your best scores, daily-challenge streak, and settings are stored locally on your device only. Deleting the app deletes them.

4. Sharing run cards

The share feature generates an image of your score on your device and hands it to the iOS share sheet. Nothing is shared unless you choose an app to share it with.

5. Children

The game does not collect personal information from anyone, including children.

6. Your rights

Since we hold no data about you, there is nothing for us to access, correct, or delete. If you have any privacy question, contact chaotic-clock@proton.me and we will do our best to help.

7. Changes

If this policy changes, the new version will be published at this address with an updated date above.
